translation: >-
  Wash the whole, cleaned grains many times. Soak jasmine-white rice, then put three times as much water as rice in a copper or earthen pot and cover it with a dense clean cloth. Bring the water to a boil over a smokeless fire, add the rice, and stir. Test grains repeatedly. When they are soft but retain a slight firm core, add milk or ghee, remove from the fire, drain the starchy water, and let the remaining moisture dry in the retained heat.
description: >-

**Ingredients**

* 300 g aged basmati or another fragrant long-grain rice  
* 1.5 L water, plus more for washing and soaking  
* 15 g ghee, or 60 ml whole milk  
* ½ tsp fine salt (optional; not specified in the text)

**Method**

1. Wash the rice until the water is nearly clear, then soak it for 30 minutes and drain.  
2. Bring 1.5 L water to a rolling boil in a large saucepan. Add the rice and stir once.  
3. Boil uncovered for 6–9 minutes, testing frequently, until the grains are tender but retain a faint firm centre.  
4. Drain immediately through a fine colander. Return the rice to the warm pan, add the ghee or milk, cover, and leave off the heat for 8 minutes.  
5. Fluff gently and serve hot.

**Notes**

* The source gives a water-to-rice ratio of three to one, but because the liquid is drained, a larger modern volume makes scorching less likely.  
* The initial instruction to cover the pot with cloth probably refers to the vessel while heating or to a cloth-bound lid; do not place loose cloth near a flame.
