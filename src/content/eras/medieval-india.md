The recipes here come from the court cooking of medieval India—the food of a royal kitchen, rich in meat, dairy, fried pastries, and sweets, set down as part of a king's idea of what a well-run court should know.

So far the section rests on a single source, the *Mānasollāsa*, a twelfth-century Sanskrit encyclopedia of kingship whose chapter on food is one of the earliest detailed records of Indian cooking we have. Its own page covers the work and its author in more detail.
