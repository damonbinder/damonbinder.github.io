name: Vinidarius
period: c. 500
---


The full title of the work *Apici Excerpta a Vinidario Viro Inlustri*, or the "Extracts of Apicius by the illustrius Vinidarius". We don't know who Vinidarius was but the name appears to be Gothic, while *vir inlustri* implies he was from the top senatorial grade. The combination suggests a composition date of 490-540, when the Ostrogothic Kingdom ruled over Italy. The sole copy of the work survives in the *Codex Salmasianus*, which itself dates to the 8th century.

Most recipes in *Vinidarius* would not be out of place in *Apicius*, though linguistically they show more proto-Romance features. A few recipes are show close parallels—compare recipes 8, 13, and 19 with *Apicius* 10.1.1, 4.2.27, and 4.2.28—but for the most part the recipes are distinct. Whatever "Apicius" was abridged to make the collection, it was not the *Apicius* that survives. 

The Latin text comes from the Grocock and Grainger edition of *Apicius*, which is the best source for more information on the text and contains an English translation; the one below is by Claude.
