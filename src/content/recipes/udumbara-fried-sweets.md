**Ingredients**

* 200 g maṇḍaka dough  
* 150 g cooled kīsāra  
* 500 ml neutral oil, for frying

**Method**

1. Divide the dough and kīsāra each into 10 portions.  
2. Flatten a piece of dough, place the filling in the centre, and pinch the dough closed around it.  
3. Roll gently into a small oval or fig shape.  
4. Heat the oil to 175°C and fry in batches until golden, 3–4 minutes.  
5. Drain and serve warm.

**Notes**

* The connection between udumbara and kīsāra is inferential; the text may instead refer to another filling lost in compression.  
* *Udumbara* means a cluster fig, perhaps describing the finished shape rather than an ingredient.
