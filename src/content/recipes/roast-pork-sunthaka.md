## Ingredients

* 1 kg skin-on pork belly or fatty pork shoulder  
* 20 ml lime juice or mild vinegar (optional sour pre-cooking)  
* 2 tsp fine rock salt  
* 1 tsp ground black pepper  
* 15 g ghee, melted

## Method

1. Cut the pork into 4 cm cubes or thick strips. For the sour variant, toss with the lime juice and half the salt and refrigerate for 2 hours.  
2. Thread the pieces onto metal skewers, leaving small gaps between them.  
3. Grill over medium-hot charcoal or under a hot broiler, turning frequently and brushing with ghee, until browned and the fat begins to drip, about 15–25 minutes depending on thickness.  
4. Cook pork belly until tender and at least 71°C in the centre; shoulder may be cooked to a higher temperature for tenderness.  
5. Sprinkle with the remaining salt and pepper and serve immediately.

**Notes**

* The carcass-cleaning instructions are omitted from the modern method; use commercially butchered meat.  
* The word *śuṇṭhaka* here is a cut or preparation name and does not mean dried ginger.
