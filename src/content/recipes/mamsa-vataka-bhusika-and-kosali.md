**Ingredients**

* 600 g finely minced lamb, goat, or pork  
* ¼ tsp asafoetida dissolved in 30 ml water  
* 1½ tsp salt  
* 1 tsp ground cumin  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper  
* ½ tsp ground coriander  
* 20 g ghee, for pan-cooking  
* 500 ml oil, for bhūṣikās  
* 250 g maṇḍaka dough, for kośalīs

**Method**

1. Mix or process the meat with the asafoetida-water, salt, cumin, pepper, and coriander until sticky and smooth.  
2. For māṃsa-vaṭakas, form 12 balls. Brown in ghee, add 100 ml water, cover, and cook until done.  
3. For bhūṣikās, form smaller balls and deep-fry at 175°C until browned and cooked through.  
4. For kośalīs, enclose small portions of meat in thin dough, seal, and bake at 220°C for 12–18 minutes, until the dough is browned and the filling cooked.

**Notes**

* The source describes a filled centre but does not identify a separate filling; the reconstruction simply folds additional mince around a compact core.  
* Check the centre of every meatball for doneness.
