**Ingredients**

* 150 g cooked fish  
* 150 g cooked chicken  
* 150 g cooked pork belly  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper  
* 1 tsp lovage  
* 50 ml fish sauce  
* 300 ml white wine  
* 150 ml *passum*  
* 30 ml olive oil  
* 2 Tb cornstarch, made into a slurry  
* 3 eggs  
* 90 g pine nuts  
* 16x 6 inch flour tortillas (about 400 g)

**Method**

1. Finely dice the cooked fish, chicken, and pork belly and set to one side.  
2. Place the pepper, lovage, fish sauce, wine, *passum*, and olive oil in a large saucepan. Bring to a simmer and cook gently for a few minutes.  
3. Add the cornstarch slurry to the saucepan, and continue to cook until it thickens.  
4. Add the diced meat, then beat the eggs and add those too. Continue cooking a gentle heat until the mixture becomes very thick while still remaining spreadable.  
5. To assemble the dish, place a tortilla on a plate. Spoon some of the meat mixture over the tortilla, sprinkle on some pine nuts, and then place another tortilla on top to complete one layer. Next add another bottom tortilla, more mixture, pine nuts, and another top tortilla, to form another layer. Keep going until you have used all the tortillas, and serve, with each layer forming a single serving. 

**Notes**

* While the original recipe calls for the recipe to be assembled in a dish and then turned over, I don’t find this is necessary to form a stack. The stack itself is decorative and not strictly necessary for the dish; a more pragmatic approach would be to simply fill each tortilla up and fold like a quesadilla.
