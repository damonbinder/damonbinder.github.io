  Scale large fish and cut them into pieces; leave small fish whole. Remove head, tail, fins, and intestines. Rub the fish with oil and salt until the slime and fish smell disappear, wash in turmeric-water, and drain in cloth. Cook washed pieces in tamarind souring liquid, scatter wheat flour over all sides, and fry in hot oil until brown. Season with cardamom, black pepper, and rock salt.

The oil-and-salt rub, turmeric rinse, and tamarind simmer didn't add much, so I'd recommend skipping them unless you want to be maximally authentic.

## Ingredients

* 500 g firm white fish fillets or steaks  
* 2 tsp salt, divided  
* *(Optional)* ½ tsp turmeric  
* *(Optional)* 30 g tamarind concentrate  
* 50 g wheat flour  
* Neutral oil, for the rub shallow-frying  
* ¼ tsp ground cardamom  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper

## Method

1. *(Optional)* Rub the fish with 15 ml oil and 1 tsp salt for 1–2 minutes. Rinse briefly in water mixed with turmeric, then pat completely dry.  
2. *(Optional)* Dilute the tamarind in 150 ml water. Simmer the fish in this liquid for 2–4 minutes, just until the exterior firms, then drain and cool.  
3. Dust every surface with wheat flour.  
4. Shallow-fry over medium-high heat, turning once, until browned and cooked through.  
5. Grind the cardamom, pepper, and remaining salt together and sprinkle over the hot fish.
