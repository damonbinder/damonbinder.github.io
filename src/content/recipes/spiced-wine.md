*Mulsum*, or honey mixed with wine, was a popular Roman aperitif. When mixed with pepper and other spices, the result is a *conditum*. What makes this *conditum paradoxum*, or "surprising", is unclear. 

The original recipe is very sweet by modern standards, but comparable with other surviving recipes for *mulsum* and *conditum*. Following the modern recipe in [The Classical Cookbook](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0892363940?lv=shuf&channelId=500&plpRedirect=mhFallback), I've cut down on the honey to make something more palatable to modern tastes. 

## Ingredients

* 1 bottle dry white wine  
* 150g honey  
* ½ tsp ground pepper  
* 1 tejpat leaf 
* Pinch saffron powder  
* 1 fresh date, with roasted stone
* Pinch mastic

## Method

1. Pit the date and roast the stone in the oven for 10 minutes  
2. Put 150 ml of wine and all of the honey in a saucepan and bring to the boil  
3. Remove from the heat and add the seasonings, including both the date flesh and roasted date pit.  
4. Once the wine-honey mixture is cold, add the rest of the wine and allow to stand overnight. Strain through a fine sieve and serve.
