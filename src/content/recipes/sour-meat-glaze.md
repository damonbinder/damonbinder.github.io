**Ingredients**

* 750 g cooked roast pork, lamb, or goat, cut into bite-sized pieces  
* 100 g plain yoghurt  
* 30 g tamarind concentrate  
* 100 ml pomegranate juice  
* 30 ml lemon juice  
* 1 tsp ground coriander  
* 1 tsp ground cumin  
* ½ tsp turmeric  
* 15 g fresh ginger, grated  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper  
* ¼ tsp asafoetida  
* 1½ tsp rock salt  
* 20 ml sesame oil or neutral oil

**Method**

1. Whisk together the yoghurt, tamarind, pomegranate juice, lemon juice, spices, salt, and oil.  
2. For a smooth sauce, strain through a fine sieve.  
3. Cook in a heavy pan over low heat, stirring, until reduced by about one-third.  
4. Add the cooked meat and simmer gently for 15–25 minutes, adding a splash of water if necessary, until the sauce clings to the pieces.  
5. Finish with an additional pinch of asafoetida dissolved in a teaspoon of hot ghee if desired.

**Notes**

* The ingredient *dantaśaṭha* and several named fumigants are uncertain and omitted.  
* The sauce should be thick and penetrating, not a large-volume curry gravy.
