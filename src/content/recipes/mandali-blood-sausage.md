**Ingredients**

* 500 ml food-grade pig or lamb blood  
* 200 g pork back fat or lamb fat, finely diced  
* 40 g lemon or citron pulp, finely chopped  
* 30 ml ginger juice  
* 1 tsp ground cumin  
* 1 tsp ground coriander  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper  
* ¼ tsp asafoetida  
* 2 tsp rock salt  
* 2 m cleaned sausage casing

**Method**

1. Keep all ingredients cold. Whisk the blood with the citrus, ginger, spices, and salt, then fold in the diced fat.  
2. Fill the casing loosely, leaving room for expansion. Tie into rings or a single coil.  
3. Poach in water held at 80–85°C until firm and the centre reaches at least 72°C, about 25–40 minutes depending on thickness.  
4. Cool briefly, then grill or roast until the casing is browned.

**Notes**

* The source heats the suspended coils over embers; controlled poaching followed by grilling greatly reduces bursting.  
* *Dantaśaṭha* juice is uncertain and omitted.  
* Use only inspected culinary blood and properly prepared casings.
