A reduced mustard offal stew combining intestine, dark organ meat, fat, and muscle meat. Liver is used as the most plausible practical interpretation of kālakhaṇḍa.

## Ingredients

* 250 g cleaned lamb intestine or tripe  
* 250 g lamb liver  
* 250 g lamb shoulder  
* 100 g lamb fat or fatty belly  
* 30 g mustard paste  
* 30 ml ginger juice  
* 30 ml tamarind concentrate diluted in 100 ml water  
* ¼ tsp asafoetida  
* 1½ tsp salt  
* 600 ml water

## Method

1. Clean and blanch the intestine or tripe according to the butcher’s instructions, then cut all meats into 2 cm pieces.  
2. Mix with the mustard paste, water, ginger juice, tamarind, asafoetida, and salt.  
3. Simmer gently until the intestine is tender, adding water if needed.  
4. Uncover and reduce until only a small amount of thick sauce remains.

**Notes**

* Kālakhaṇḍa may be liver or another dark organ; the identification is not secure.  
* Buy cleaned offal from a reputable butcher and observe conservative cooking times.
