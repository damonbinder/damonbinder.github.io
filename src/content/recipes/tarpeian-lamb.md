---
title: Tarpeian Lamb
source: Apicius 8.6.8
originalLang: Latin
original: >-
  hedus siue agnus crudus: oleo piper fricabis et asparges fores salem purum
  multo cum coriandri semen, in furnum mittis, assatum inferes.
translation: >-
  Tarpeian kid or lamb, raw: rub it with oil and pepper, and sprinkle the
  outside with pure salt together with plenty of coriander seed, put it in the
  oven, and serve it roasted.
category: Meat
subcategory: Lamb and Goat
order: 51
---

**Ingredients**

* 1 kg boneless lamb roast  
* Pepper  
* 1 small onion, finely minced or pulverised, divided  
* 1 tsp rue, divided  
* 1 tsp savory, divided  
* ½ tsp thyme  
* 4 Tb fish sauce, divided  
* 240 ml white wine  
* 2 Tb date syrup  
* 2 Tb grape syrup  
* Olive oil

**Method**

1. Mix together half of the rue, savory, onion, and fish sauce, along with the thyme and a generous grind of pepper, and rub over the lamb. Marinate for 4–8 hours.  
2. When ready to cook, preheat the oven to 180C/350F. Coat the bottom of a deep roasting dish or dutch oven with a little olive oil, then place on the lamb and roast uncovered until cooked to desired doneness—around 1 hr to reach medium rare.  
3. When the lamb has 10 minutes left to cook, prepare the sauce. In a saucepan, mix together the remaining rue, savory, onion, and fish sauce along with the white wine, date syrup, grape syrup, and a tablespoon of olive oil. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 5 minutes.  
4. Once the lamb is done, pull from the oven. Pour over the sauce, mixing together with the drippings from the lamb.   
5. Add a generous grinding of pepper, then allow the meat to rest, covered, for 10 minutes before carving.
