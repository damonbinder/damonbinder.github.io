---
title: Boiled Cabbage with Spiced “Dry Soup”
source: From Hierophilus, January
category: Vegetables
subcategory: Cabbage
order: 123
---

**Ingredients**

* 600 g green or white cabbage, cored and cut into 1 cm strips  
* 15 mL extra‑virgin olive oil  
* 15 mL fish sauce  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper  
* ½ tsp ground cinnamon  
* A pinch ground clove  
* A pinch ground spikenard, to taste  
* 1 Tb honey  
* Salt


**Method**

1. Put the sliced cabbage into a saucepan and add cold water to just cover. Bring to a boil, then simmer 5–7 min until tender.  
2. Tip the cabbage into a sieve over a bowl to catch the cooking liquor. Measure out 350–400 mL liquor for the dry soup.  
3. Return the drained cabbage to the warm pan. Add olive oil and fish sauce; toss over low heat 1–2 min so it glistens but doesn’t fry. Transfer to a serving dish.  
4. Make the dry soup. In a small saucepan combine the reserved liquor with pepper, cinnamon, clove, and spikenard. Simmer gently 2–4 min until aromatic. Sweeten with honey to balance, and add a pinch of salt only if needed. Strain for a clear finish.  
5. Present the cabbage dressed with oil and fish sauce. Serve the hot spiced liquor in small cups alongside as the “dry soup.”
