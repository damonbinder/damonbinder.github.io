**Ingredients**

* 1 batch fermented iḍerikā batter  
* 700 ml neutral oil, for frying  
* Water, for wetting the hands

**Method**

1. Heat the oil to 175°C.  
2. Wet one hand and spread about 50 g batter into a thick disc on the palm or on a piece of baking parchment.  
3. Make five or seven small holes with a wet fingertip or chopstick.  
4. Slide carefully into the oil and fry until deep golden to reddish brown, 3–5 minutes, turning once.  
5. Drain on a rack and serve hot.

**Notes**

* A single central hole is easier, but the source explicitly describes five or seven holes.  
* Use a thermometer: thick urad batter can brown outside before the centre is cooked.
