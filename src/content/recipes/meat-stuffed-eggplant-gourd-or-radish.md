**Ingredients**

* 4 small eggplants, or 2 small ridge gourds, or 4 large daikon sections  
* 400 g seasoned meat paste from the māṃsa-vaṭaka recipe  
* 30 ml oil  
* 150 ml water or light stock  
* ½ tsp salt

**Method**

1. Cut a cap from each vegetable and hollow out the centre while keeping the walls intact.  
2. Fill firmly with the seasoned meat and replace the cap, securing it with a toothpick if needed.  
3. Heat the oil in a covered pan and brown the vegetables on several sides.  
4. Add the water or stock and salt, cover, and cook gently until both vegetable and filling are fully cooked, 20–35 minutes.

**Notes**

* For daikon, parboil the hollowed sections for 5 minutes before filling.  
* *Kośātakī* is usually identified as a ridged or angled gourd.
