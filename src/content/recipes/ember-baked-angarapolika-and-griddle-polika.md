## Ingredients

* 1 batch maṇḍaka dough  
* Rice flour or wheat flour, for dusting  
* Ghee, for serving

## Method

1. For griddle polikās, divide the dough into 8 balls, dust generously, and roll to 10–12 cm rounds about 5 mm thick.  
2. Cook on a medium-hot griddle for 1–2 minutes per side until mottled brown and cooked through.  
3. For an ember-style version in a modern oven, heat a baking stone or heavy tray at 250°C. Place the thick rounds directly on the hot surface and bake for 4–6 minutes, turning once.  
4. Brush lightly with ghee and serve warm.

**Notes**

* Actual burial in wood embers is possible only with clean hardwood coals and careful ash removal; the hot-stone method is safer indoors.  
* The term *polikā* is related to later *poli* breads but the recipe here does not specify a filling.
