Fresh acid-set curds are kneaded with rice flour, fried, and syruped. The result falls between a fried paneer sweet and a rice-flour fritter.

## Ingredients

* 1 L whole milk  
* 200 ml cultured buttermilk, plus more if needed  
* 50 g rice flour  
* 100 g ghee, for shallow-frying  
* 150 g sugar  
* 150 ml water  
* ½ tsp ground cardamom

## Method

1. Bring the milk just to a boil. Lower the heat and add the buttermilk gradually until the curds separate clearly from the whey.  
2. Strain through cloth, rinse briefly with cool water, and hang for 30 minutes.  
3. Knead the curds with the rice flour until smooth. Shape into small discs, balls, or rings.  
4. Cook the sugar and water for 5–7 minutes to make a light syrup; add the cardamom.  
5. Shallow-fry the shapes in ghee over medium-low heat until golden, then transfer directly to the warm syrup.  
6. Soak for at least 30 minutes before serving.

**Notes**

* The source says “fry in ghee”; shallow-frying is more economical and reduces the chance of disintegration.  
* Use additional buttermilk or a little lemon juice only if the milk fails to curdle fully.
