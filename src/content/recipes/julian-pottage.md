  Julian pottage is cooked thus: soak cleaned emmer grits, cook them, bring to
  a boil. When it has boiled, add oil; when it has thickened, bind it
  carefully. Add two cooked brains and half a pound of meat pounded smooth as
  if for forcemeat balls: grind this with the brains and put into a pot. Grind
  pepper, lovage, and fennel seed, pour on fish sauce and a little wine. Put
  into the pot over the brains and meat; when it has boiled enough, mix with

## Ingredients

* 200 g semolina  
* 1 Tb olive oil  
* 500 g mince  
* 1 tsp pepper  
* 1 tsp lovage  
* 1 tsp ground fennel seed  
* 45 ml fish sauce  
* 150 ml white wine

## Method

1. Place the semolina in a large saucepan with 1 L of water and the olive oil. Bring to a simmer and cook until thickened, approximately 5 minutes.  
2. Place the mince in a dutch oven and fry on high heat until cooked through.  
3. Add the spices, fish sauce, and wine to the frying pan. Bring to a simmer. Then add the semolina. Stir thoroughly to combine and reheat the semolina, and serve.
