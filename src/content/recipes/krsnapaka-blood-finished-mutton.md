A dark mutton stew thickened and enriched with fresh blood. The recipe is practical only where inspected food-grade blood is legally available.

## Ingredients

* 750 g boneless mutton or lamb shoulder, cut into 2 cm pieces  
* 200 ml food-grade lamb or pig blood  
* 200 ml tamarind water  
* 100 ml pomegranate juice  
* 1 tsp ground cumin  
* 1 tsp ground coriander  
* ½ tsp turmeric  
* ½ tsp ground dried ginger  
* ¼ tsp asafoetida  
* 1½ tsp rock salt  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper  
* ¼ tsp ground cardamom  
* Optional: a speck of food-grade edible camphor  
* 20 g ghee

## Method

1. Whisk ½ tsp salt into the blood and strain through a fine sieve. Keep refrigerated.  
2. Combine the meat, tamarind water, pomegranate juice, cumin, coriander, turmeric, dried ginger, asafoetida, and remaining salt. Simmer covered until the meat is tender.  
3. Lower the heat. Whisk the blood again and add it gradually while stirring.  
4. Cook gently for 8–10 minutes, stirring, until thickened and fully cooked; do not leave the blood merely warmed.  
5. Finish with ghee, black pepper, cardamom, and optional camphor.

**Notes**

* Use only blood sold for culinary use and keep it cold until cooking.  
* The source’s blood-processing instructions reflect immediate slaughter and should not be reproduced outside regulated conditions.
