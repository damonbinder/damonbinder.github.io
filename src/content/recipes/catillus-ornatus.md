## Ingredients

* 400g lettuce  
* 50 ml white wine  
* About 250 g white flour  
* ½ tsp salt  
* 30 g lard  
* Pepper  
* Olive oil, for frying

## Method

1. Wash the lettuce leaves and blend with the white wine to form a puree. Pour the lettuce puree into a fine mesh strainer, and mix with a spoon to drain as much liquid as possible.   
2. Place the drained lettuce puree in a large mixing bowl. Add most of the flour and mix to incorporate, then keep adding flour and kneading until the mixture comes together into a smooth dough. Add the lard and a generous amount of pepper, then knead for a few more minutes until incorporated.  
3. On a floured bench top, roll out the dough as thin as you can. Cut into long strips.  
4. Pour enough olive oil into a frying pan to create a layer several centimetres deep, and heat until shimmering. Add a batch of the fritters and fry until crisp and golden, transferring to a draining rack or paper-towel lined plate once done. Repeat until all the fritters are cooked, adding additional olive oil to the frying pan as needed. 

**Notes**

* These can be eaten by themselves as a snack, sprinkled with some extra salt and pepper. They can also be used as cracker for dips or vegetable purees.
