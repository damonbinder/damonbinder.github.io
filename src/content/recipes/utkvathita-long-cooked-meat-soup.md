**Ingredients**

* 1.5 kg bone-in lamb or goat shoulder, breast, neck, and ribs  
* 3 L water  
* 40 g tamarind pulp or 60 ml lemon juice  
* ½ tsp asafoetida  
* 2½ tsp rock salt, plus more to taste

**Method**

1. Place the meat and bones in a large heavy pot with the water.  
2. Bring slowly to a boil and skim the surface.  
3. Add the tamarind, asafoetida, and salt. Simmer very gently, partly covered, for 3–4 hours, until the meat falls easily from the bones.  
4. Remove the bones, return the meat to the broth, and adjust salt and sourness.

**Notes**

* The source does not specify skimming, but it produces a cleaner modern broth.  
* The term *utkvāthita* emphasizes prolonged boiling or decoction.
