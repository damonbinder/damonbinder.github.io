**Ingredients**

* 500 g full-fat plain yoghurt or cultured curd  
* For mathita: no added water  
* For udaśvit: 250 ml water  
* For takra: 125 ml water  
* For spiced takra: 10 g fresh ginger, grated; ½ tsp cumin; pinch asafoetida; ¼ tsp cardamom; ¾ tsp rock salt

**Method**

1. Churn or blend the yoghurt until the butterfat begins to gather. Remove any butter that separates.  
2. For mathita, serve the remaining churned liquid undiluted.  
3. For udaśvit, whisk in water equal to half the curd’s volume.  
4. For takra, whisk in water equal to one-quarter the curd’s volume.  
5. For spiced takra, briefly heat the cumin and asafoetida in a teaspoon of ghee, cool, then whisk into the takra with ginger, cardamom, and salt.

**Notes**

* The traditional process begins with set curd and removes butter; simply diluting store-bought low-fat yoghurt gives a less exact result.
