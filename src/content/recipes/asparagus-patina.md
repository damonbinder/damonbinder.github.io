---
title: Asparagus Patina
source: Apicius 4.2.5
originalLang: Latin
original: >-
  aliter patina de asparagis frigida: accipies asparagos purgatos, in mortario
  fricabis; aqua suffundes, perfricabis; per colum colabis, et mittes
  fecitulas curatas; teres in mortario piperis scripulos sex, adicies
  liquamen; fricabis; uini ciatum I passi ciatum I; mittes in caccabum olei
  uncias tres; illic ferueant. perungues patinam; in ea oua VI cum enogaro
  misces; cum suco asparagi inpones cineri calido. [mittes inpensam super
  scriptam] tunc ficeulas conpones, quoques; piper asparges et inferes.
translation: >-
  Another way, cold asparagus *patina*: take cleaned asparagus, crush it in a
  mortar; pour on water, work it thoroughly; strain through a sieve; and add
  prepared figpeckers [small birds]. Grind in the mortar six scruples of
  pepper, add fish sauce; work it; one *cyathus* of wine, one *cyathus* of
  *passum*; put three ounces of oil in a pot; let these boil there. Grease a
  *patina*; in it mix six eggs with *oenogarum*; with the asparagus juice set
  it over hot ashes. [Add the mixture written above.] Then arrange the
  figpeckers, cook it; sprinkle with pepper and serve.
category: Eggs and Patinas
subcategory: Vegetable Patinas
order: 11
---

See Sally Grainger recipe, pg 52 of *Cooking Apicius*. I omitted the asparagus garnish, which isn’t in the original. Sally extracts the asparagus flavor in wine but the original calls for water.
