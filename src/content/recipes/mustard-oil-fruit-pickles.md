A direct ancestor in principle of mustard-rich South Asian oil pickles. The reconstruction lightly cures the produce with salt before adding heated mustard oil.

## Ingredients

* 1 kg green mango, bitter gourd, firm green banana, or young jackfruit  
* 40 g salt  
* 40 g ground mustard seed  
* 250 ml mustard oil  
* 1 tsp turmeric  
* Optional: 1 tsp fenugreek seed

## Method

1. Wash and dry the produce completely. Cut into bite-sized pieces and toss with the salt. Rest in the refrigerator overnight.  
2. Drain any released liquid and spread the pieces to dry for 1 hour.  
3. Heat the mustard oil until it just begins to smoke, then cool to warm.  
4. Mix the produce with ground mustard, turmeric, optional fenugreek, and the warm oil.  
5. Pack into a clean jar, ensuring the surface is covered with oil. Refrigerate and leave for at least 5 days before eating.

**Notes**

* The source does not state heating the oil; this is a common modern treatment that mellows raw mustard oil.  
* Commercial mustard oil labelled for external use should not be used as food where regulations prohibit it.
