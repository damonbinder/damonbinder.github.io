A two-stage dish: meat is first simmered in mustard and ginger, then finished under cover in a fragrant ghee tempering.

## Ingredients

* 750 g boneless lamb or goat, cut into 4 cm pieces  
* 800 ml water  
* 2 tsp ground mustard  
* 1 tsp ground dried ginger  
* 20 g fresh ginger, sliced  
* 1 tsp fenugreek powder  
* 2 Tb chopped coriander leaves  
* 1½ tsp rock salt  
* 40 g ghee  
* 1 small onion, finely chopped  
* 3 garlic cloves, sliced  
* ¼ tsp asafoetida

## Method

1. Whisk the mustard into the water. Add the meat, dried ginger, and fresh ginger and simmer until nearly tender, 45–75 minutes.  
2. Reserve 250 ml of the cooking liquid and drain the meat.  
3. Return the meat and reserved liquid to the pan with fenugreek, coriander, and salt. Cook until the liquid is mostly absorbed.  
4. Heat the ghee in a separate pan. Fry the onion, garlic, and asafoetida until aromatic and lightly browned.  
5. Add the meat, cover, and cook over low heat for 8–10 minutes. Serve hot.

**Notes**

* The exact sequence in verse 1458 is textually difficult; this reconstruction preserves the stated boiling, seasoning, and final covered frying.  
* The dish-name reading is uncertain.
