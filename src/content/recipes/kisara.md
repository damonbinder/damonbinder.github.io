A sweet, rich preparation of coarse wheat grain toasted in ghee and spiced with cardamom and black pepper. The source calls for a deliberately coarse grain — the gritty fraction winnowed from flour — which keeps the grains distinct rather than collapsing into a smooth paste.

## Ingredients

* 150 g coarse semolina (coarse rava/sooji) or dalia (cracked wheat) — not fine semolina; see note  
* about 250 ml whole milk  
* 60 g ghee  
* 75 g sugar  
* ½ tsp ground cardamom  
* ¼ tsp finely ground black pepper  
* Pinch of salt

## Method

1. Moisten the coarse grain with a few tablespoons of the milk — just enough to dampen it, not a soak.  
2. Melt the ghee in a heavy pan over medium-low heat. Add the moistened grain and cook, stirring constantly, until it smells nutty and the grains stay loose and separate.  
3. Add the remaining milk a little at a time, stirring after each addition so it is absorbed before the next. Stop early for a drier, granular sweet, or keep adding liquid for a thick porridge.  
4. Stir in the sugar, cardamom, pepper, and salt, and cook a few minutes more.  
5. Serve warm in small portions.

**Notes**

* **Grain matters most.** The source calls for coarse wheat particles winnowed from flour, and grain size is what governs the texture. Fine semolina (standard fine sooji, farina, Cream of Wheat) absorbs liquid fast and welds into a lump in hot ghee — use a coarse rava, or dalia/cracked wheat, so the grains stay distinct. Bulgur works as a fallback but is par-cooked, so it cooks faster and comes out nuttier.  
* **Keep the milk light at the start.** A heavy pre-soak swells the grain into a sticky mass that fuses on contact with the ghee; dampen lightly, toast, then add the rest of the milk gradually.  
* Dalia is whole wheat and takes more liquid and longer to soften than semolina, tending toward the porridge end.  
* The black pepper is explicit and should remain perceptible, though not dominant.
