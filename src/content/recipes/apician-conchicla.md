translation: >-
  *Conchicla* in the Apician manner: take a clean Cumaean dish, in which you
  will cook the peas, and to which you add sliced Lucanian sausages, little
  pork forcemeats, [pieces of] meat, and ham. Grind pepper, lovage, oregano,
  dill, dried onion, and fresh coriander; moisten with fish sauce; temper it
  with wine and fish sauce. Put it into the Cumaean dish, to which you add
  oil, prick it all over so that it drinks up the oil, and cook over a low
  fire so that it simmers, then serve.

**Ingredients**

* 500g split peas  
* 300g pork shoulder, cooked and diced  
* 300g cooked sausages, sliced  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper  
* 1 tsp lovage  
* 1½ tsp oregano  
* ½ tsp ground dill seed  
* 1 small onion, finely diced  
* A handful coriander leaves, finely chopped  
* 60 ml fish sauce  
* 60 ml white wine  
* 30 ml olive oil

**Method**

1. Place the split peas in a saucepan along with 1500 ml of water. Bring to a boil, and then cook covered for about 75 minutes, or until the peas become mushy and very thick.  
2. Add the remaining ingredients to the saucepan. Stir and cook for an additional 10 minutes, then remove from the heat and serve.
