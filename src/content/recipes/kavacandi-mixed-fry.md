Kavacandī is deliberately miscellaneous: a richly seasoned mixed fry using meat, vegetables, sprouts, and pulse cakes. The reconstruction keeps the dish varied but coherent.

## Ingredients

* 400 g boneless lamb, pork, or goat, cut into 2 cm pieces  
* 150 g eggplant, cut into 2 cm pieces  
* 150 g daikon radish, cut into 2 cm pieces  
* 1 small onion, cut into wedges  
* 25 g fresh ginger, cut into small pieces  
* 100 g mung-bean sprouts  
* 6 small urad vaṭakas or plain lentil fritters, quartered  
* 30 ml oil  
* ½ tsp turmeric  
* 1 tsp cumin  
* 1 tsp coriander  
* ½ tsp black pepper  
* 1½ tsp salt  
* 150 ml sour meat glaze  
* 2 Tb chopped fresh herbs or tender greens

## Method

1. Heat the oil in a wide pan and brown the meat thoroughly.  
2. Add the onion, ginger, radish, and eggplant and cook until beginning to soften.  
3. Add the turmeric, cumin, coriander, pepper, and salt, followed by the sour glaze.  
4. Cover and cook gently until the meat and vegetables are tender.  
5. Fold in the mung sprouts, vaṭakas, and herbs. Cook uncovered until the sauce is reduced and the sprouts are just tender.

**Notes**

* *Meṣaka* and *kāsamarda* are uncertain plant terms; fresh herbs and tender greens stand in for them.  
* The name is explained by the source itself as referring to the dish’s many ingredients.
