The *Mānasollāsa*, or, loosely translated "joy of the mind", is an Sanskrit text composed by the [Western Chalukya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Chalukya_Empire) king [Someshvara III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Someshvara_III). The work is an encyclopedia covering all topics relevant for kingship. 

Food is covered in Book 3, Chapter 13, titled *Annabhoga* or "enjoyment of foods", proceeded by a chapter on raising children and followed by chapters on drinking water, foot messages, vehicles, and umbrellas. The roughly 70 recipes cover the full range of dishes found in the royal court, from rice and pulses to fried pastries and sweets. While not the oldest surviving Indian work devoted to cookery — that distinction belongs to the *Lokopakāra*, c. 1025 — it is the first to contain an extensive range of meat dishes, including recipes for turtle and *mūṣaka*, probably the [bandicoot rat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greater_bandicoot_rat).
