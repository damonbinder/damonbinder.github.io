## Ingredients

* 100 g fragrant short-grain rice, or 80 g millet  
* 1 L whole milk  
* 100 ml double cream (optional, especially when using cow’s milk)  
* 75 g sugar  
* ¼ tsp ground cardamom  
* Pinch of salt

## Method

1. Rinse the grain. Combine it with the milk and cream in a heavy saucepan.  
2. Bring slowly to a simmer, then cook over very low heat for 45–60 minutes, stirring frequently and scraping the base.  
3. When the grain is completely tender and the pudding is thick enough to mound softly on a spoon, add the sugar, cardamom, and salt.  
4. Cook for another 5 minutes and serve warm or chilled.

**Notes**

* Sugar and cardamom are plausible additions for the “sweet-smelling” result but are not explicitly listed in these verses.  
* For a closer grain alternative, use barnyard millet for *śyāmāka* or foxtail millet for *kaṅgu*.
