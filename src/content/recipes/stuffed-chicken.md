## Ingredients

* 1 whole chicken, about 2 kg  
* Salt  
* Olive oil

*For stuffing*

* 150 g cooked pork shoulder, finely diced  
* 70 g bulgur wheat  
* 3 eggs  
* 60 g pinenuts  
* ½  tsp ground black pepper  
* ½  tsp whole black peppercorns  
* 1½ tsp ginger  
* 1 tsp lovage  
* 30 ml fish sauce  
* 30 ml olive oil

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 190C/375F.   
2. Place the bulgur wheat, pour over boiling water and leave to soak  
3. Galantine the chicken—look up a video explaining how to do that  
4. Thoroughly drain the bulgur wheat, then mix with the remaining stuffing ingredients.   
5. Stuff the chicken, securing it with butcher's twine. Place in a roasting tin, brush the skin with a little olive oil and salt, then roast for about 75 minutes or until the meat reaches 74C/165F..  
6. Remove from the oven and allow to rest for 10 minutes, then carve and serve.

**Notes**

* When I attempt this the galantine was a bit messy; it didn’t totally fail but I think I could have been more careful to not cut the skin more than needed  
* The main issue was that the egg largely leaked out of the stuffing, along with the salt / seasoning to some extent. Plausibly it could have done with more salt anyway.  
* O3’s suggestions were to:  
  * Make sure the bulgur was thoroughly drained after soaking  
  * Maybe reduce to 2 eggs  
  * Maybe set the stuffing by cooking slightly or microwaving for 20 s to get to loose custard  
* From the recipe itself it isn’t clear you have to galantine it  
* The Classical Cookbook has a recipe for this with fairly different quantities, maybe should try theirs instead
