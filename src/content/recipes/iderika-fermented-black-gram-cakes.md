## Ingredients

* 250 g skinned urad dal  
* 120–180 ml water  
* 1 tsp fine salt  
* ¼ tsp asafoetida  
* ½ tsp ground cumin  
* ½ tsp ground black pepper  
* 20 g ghee

## Method

1. Soak the urad dal for 6 hours, drain, and grind with enough water to make a very thick, smooth batter.  
2. Beat the batter vigorously for 5 minutes to incorporate air. Mix in the salt and half the asafoetida.  
3. Cover and ferment in a warm place for 8–16 hours, until slightly sour and aerated.  
4. Grease small ramekins or an idli tray, fill two-thirds full, cover, and steam for 10–12 minutes.  
5. Cool until the cakes are white and firm. Melt the ghee with the cumin and remaining asafoetida, spoon it over the cakes, and finish with pepper.

**Notes**

* The text does not explicitly say “steam,” but covered moulds strongly suggest moist enclosed cooking.  
* The batter contains no rice in the source, unlike many modern idlis.
